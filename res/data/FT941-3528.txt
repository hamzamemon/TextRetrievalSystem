VM, the Italian diesel engine manufacturer, has won a contract worth more
than L20bn (Pounds 8m) to supply six-cylinder engines for the Swiss army's
new Duro troop transporter. The contract is with Bucher Guyer of
Niederveningen, which will produce 2,000 vehicles over the next four years.
VM, which is based near Bologna and supplies Alfa Romeo, Ford and Rover, is
to provide the Ecosystem Power (EP) 638 LI engine which has a maximum speed
of 100km per hour and meets European Union emission standards due to come
into force at the end of 1995.
