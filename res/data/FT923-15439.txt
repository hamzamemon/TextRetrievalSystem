AETNA Life &amp; Casualty, one of the largest US insurance companies, warned
yesterday that it will take a Dollars 45.7m charge against its
second-quarter earnings,because of indirect involvement in the Olympia &amp;
York situation, writes Nikki Tait in New York
The insurer faces obligations under a surety bond issued by its Aetna
Casualty and Surety Company subsidiary, causing Aetna to pay Dollars 20.75m
to holders of Eurobonds issued by O&amp;Y to finance its 55 Water Street
property in New York yesterday.
