SEIZED DRUGS money will be paid to police and customs to boost the battle
against international traffickers, Mr Kenneth Baker the home secretary
announced last night.
Assets seized under international confiscation agreements will finance a new
multi-million pound fund also available for anti-drugs education or
treatment and rehabilitation of addicts, he told the Association of Chief
Police Officers drugs conference in Preston.
The fund, due to be established in April 1992, will be in addition to
resources already devoted to combating drug misuse and trafficking.
